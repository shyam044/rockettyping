Keyboard Shortcuts for Mac, Windows, Linux

📅 June 16, 2026⏱ 13 min read✍️ Rocket Typing Team
Master the shortcuts that keep your hands on the keyboard and your workflow moving quickly on Mac, Windows, and Linux.

Why shortcuts matter for typing

Every time you reach for the mouse, your flow breaks. Keyboard shortcuts let you stay in the typing zone and move faster through editing and navigation.

Learning a few high-impact shortcuts makes your typing sessions more productive and reduces the friction of repetitive tasks.

Key shortcuts for Mac

Start with ⌘C, ⌘V, ⌘X, ⌘Z, and ⌘F. Add ⌘Tab for app switching and ⌘W to close windows. These shortcuts make text editing and workspace control much faster.

For text navigation, learn ⌥+arrow keys and ⌘←/⌘→. They help you move through sentences and lines without taking your hands off the keyboard.

Essential Windows shortcuts

Use Ctrl+C, Ctrl+V, Ctrl+X, Ctrl+Z, and Ctrl+F as your basis. Learn Alt+Tab for switching apps and Windows+arrow keys for window arrangement.

Text navigation shortcuts like Ctrl+Left/Right and Ctrl+Backspace speed up editing, while Ctrl+Shift+T can reopen a closed browser tab.

Linux shortcuts that speed work

Many Linux desktop shortcuts mirror Windows, but there are Linux-specific productivity keys like Ctrl+Alt+T for opening a terminal and Ctrl+Shift+T for new terminal tabs.

If you use terminal-based editors, learn their built-in commands too. The faster you can switch between tools, the more efficient your typing sessions become.

Platform Core shortcut Why it helps
Mac ⌘C / ⌘V Speeds copy and paste without leaving the keyboard
Windows Ctrl+Z / Ctrl+Y Makes undo and redo fast during editing
Linux Ctrl+Alt+T Opens the terminal quickly for developer workflows

Build a shortcut habit

Pick one or two shortcuts at a time and use them until they feel natural. Practice them in every session, even if it feels slower at first.

Keep a reference sheet handy for a few days. The speed payoff comes from muscle memory, not memorization alone.
