Typing Ergonomics and RSI Prevention

📅 June 16, 2026⏱ 12 min read✍️ Rocket Typing Team
Protect your hands, wrists, and shoulders with easy ergonomic typing habits. Prevent repetitive strain injury with posture, breaks, and workspace adjustments.

What is RSI?

Repetitive strain injury (RSI) is pain or discomfort caused by repeated motions, poor posture, or tension. Typing for long periods without breaks can contribute to RSI.

Preventing RSI is about reducing stress on your body with the right setup and frequent recovery.

Use ergonomic posture

Position your screen at eye level, keep your shoulders relaxed, and maintain a neutral wrist alignment. Your forearms should float comfortably above the keyboard.

Feet should be flat on the floor, and your back should be supported. Good posture reduces pressure on joints and helps you type longer with less fatigue.

Take regular breaks

Take a short break every 20–30 minutes. Stand up, stretch, and move your hands and arms to keep blood flowing and reduce stiffness.

A simple break can be enough to reset your posture and prevent tension from building up during a long session.

Ergonomic habit What it protects How to practice
Neutral wrists Wrist and hand strain Avoid bending wrists up or down while typing
Relaxed shoulders Neck and upper back tension Keep shoulders down and elbows close to the body
Regular breaks Joint stiffness Stand and stretch every 30 minutes

Stretch in between sessions

Simple stretches can relieve tension in your wrists, forearms, and shoulders. Try wrist flexor and extensor stretches, shoulder rolls, and neck turns.

Do a quick stretch every hour to keep your muscles loose and ready for the next typing block.

Use the right equipment

Choose a keyboard and mouse that feel comfortable. Split keyboards, low-profile key switches, and ergonomic mice can all reduce strain.

Even small adjustments, like a low keyboard angle or wrist pad, can make your typing feel easier over time.
